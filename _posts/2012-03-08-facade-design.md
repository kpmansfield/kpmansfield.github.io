Joe Lynes talked at <em>CIBSE Daylighting Group</em> about the challenge of educating architects in window design. Façades deliver a message—what message is being delivered by <em>Panter Hudsmith's</em> Bear Lane [development](http://www.panterhudspith.com/projects/residential/bearlane.html), apparently inspired by the Giant's Causeway?

Joe's three essential concepts for architectural students:

1. The <em>no-sky</em> line.
2. The matchbox sundial.
3.  Reflectance (<em>British Standard</em> colours).

He also made the point—the significance of which escapes me—that the concept of <em>luminous flux</em> was not introduced until after Sumpner's work was completed on inter-reflection. Kit Cuttle's paper in <em>Lighting Research and Technology</em> may help here.[^1]

---
[^1]:Cuttle C. 'Sumpner's principle: A discussion'. <em>Lighting Research and Technology</em>, Vol 23, No 2 (1991) pp99-106.
