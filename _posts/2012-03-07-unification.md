title: Unification in Mathematics
---

Very struck by a pas­sage in W. W. Sawyer’s *Pre­lude to Math­e­mat­ics* (dat­ing from 1955) con­cern­ing ‘uni­fi­ca­tion’ in mathematics:

> …hun­dreds of papers are writ­ten, each clar­i­fy­ing one facet of the truth; the sub­ject is grow­ing. … It then ceases to be nec­es­sary to read the hun­dreds of sep­a­rate con­tri­bu­tions… The var­ie­gated results are welded together into a sin­gle doc­trine… The new meth­ods hav­ing become com­mon prop­erty, new prob­lems are found which they are insuf­fi­cient to solve…new papers are pub­lished; expan­sion begins again. There always is, there always will be some­thing to learn, some­thing to conquer.
